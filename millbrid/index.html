Our Service

Lebanon’s current economic turmoil is wreaking havoc on its citizens’ purchasing power. Currency exchange rates are volatile, and long lines at banks and money transfer entities are testing people’s patience and resolve.
Transfers from abroad, traditionally a lifeline for the country’s citizens, are often cashed at lower currency rates, further exacerbating economic hardships.

Food retailers are struggling to restock their shelves at affordable prices. The scarcity of hard currency makes it all the more difficult for them to settle payments to their suppliers.

Amid this situation arises the MillionBridges initiative, bringing forth an innovative, unique and convenient system to channel remittances from Lebanese emigrants abroad to their loved ones back home. Our system is safe, secure, fast, convenient and value-based. It is also strictly regulated by a trustworthy Canadian-European financial entity.

For Lebanese abroad

MillionBridges will channel your remittance using a safe, secure and regulated system, allowing it to reach your loved ones back home instantaneously. We will deliver the exact amount of your remittance, not a euro less. If you send 200€, your loved one will receive 200€.

For Merchants (Supermarkets)

MillionBridges will provide you with hard currency (Euros), with which to settle your invoices, allowing you to restock your shelves at prices affordable to Lebanese consumers.


For Lebanese at home

MillionBridges will deliver funds sent to you from abroad immediately, and through your mobile phone. You will not have to line up at financial institutions to retrieve your funds, nor will you be charged any commissions. The money sent to you will remain in Euros, maintaining its full value, until you are ready to use it, at which point it will be converted to LBP at the most advantageous currency rate.

Keep your wallet at home, and take only your ID card and mobile phone when shopping for groceries. MillionBridges’ system is completely cashless.

Our System

MillionBridges offers a safe, secure and regulated system that is instantaneous, convenient and value preserving.

Safe, secure and regulated

We have developed a system that ensures funds are sent and received in an instant, in real time, using advanced software technology.

Funds are received in electronic voucher format, using mobile phone messaging. It is therefore convenient, hassle-free and cashless.

Remittances are received in or converted to Euros, and kept in that currency until they are spent. This ensures that value is preserved, and is not impacted by the volatility of exchange rates and inflation.

Instantaneous, convenient and value preserving

We have partnered with RevoluPay EP SL, a Toronto Stock Exchange publicly listed Payment Entity pursuant to PSD2 EU Banking Directives under Banco de España’s license number 6900.

All financial transactions are handled solely by the regulated Payment Entity. PSD2 EU Banking Directives stipulate that MillionBridges never has access to the funds sent nor received.

Funds are always kept under the legal custody of the Payment Entity, and in segregated individual accounts. The system ensures peace of mind and provides all legal guaranties to users.

How it works?

Initial steps: setting up the account and wallet

01.Create an account

The donor opens an account with MillionBridges using a valid ID (Lebanese or foreign)

02.The beneficiary’s information

He/she then provides the beneficiary’s information: name, relation, gender, date of birth, Lebanese ID number (uploads a scan), mobile phone number and email a ddress (optional).

03.Verification

An email will be sent to the beneficiary’s email address (if it is provided). Additionally, an SMS will be sent to the beneficiary’s mobile number for verification. It will be necessary to confirm the mobile number before the verification code expires. The donor will still be able to provide any missing details and/or verification at a later time.

04.Verification

Once all the information is provided and the mobile verified, the beneficiary can start receiving funds from the donor. A wallet will be created and the beneficiary will receive an SMS with an Id and a passcode to access the wallet on MillionBridges platform.

05.Additional Donor

More than one donor can send money to the same beneficiary. In this case, the additional donor will need to repeat the same process for the beneficiary. An additional wallet will be created for the beneficiary, and he/she can access each wallet separately on MillionBridges platform.

Replenishing the beneficiary’s wallet

01.Setup Complete

Now that the setup is done, the donor can replenish the wallet of the beneficiary using a valid credit card

02.The beneficiary’s wallet

The replenishment will be made and kept in the beneficiary’s wallet in Euros

03.The usage

The beneficiary can now use that amount to pay for his/her groceries in LBP at the current market exchange rate (no loss of value in the currency exchange process)

Using MillionBridges to purchase food

01.Shopping List

Once the funds are in the wallet, the beneficiary can go to a partner supermarket (embed list of partner supermarkets) and select his/her food & groceries items.

02.CardHolder

At the cash register, he/she announces that he/she wants to pay with MillionBridges.

03.Payement process

The purchases are put on hold, while he/she goes to the customer service counter to process the payment.

04.The customer service

At the customer service counter the beneficiary will provide his/her details and can choose to spend for his/her purchases by specifying the amounts to be deducted from each wallet (in case he/she has more than one). The clerk enters the ID and mobile number of the beneficiary into the MillionBridges website, as well as the amount to be paid in Lebanese Pounds. The system calculates the counter value in Euros at the current market exchange rate.

05.The balance

If the available funds are sufficient, the MillionBridges website then sends an activation code through SMS to the beneficiary’s mobile phone.

06.Verification Step

The beneficiary gives the code to the clerk who enters it to the MillionBridges website, obtains a payment approval, and the amount in Euros is deducted from the beneficiary’s wallet (or wallets).

07.The usage

The clerk gives a ticket to the beneficiary who presents it to the cash register to receive his/her purchases.

FAQ

1. How is MillionBridges helpful for Lebanon?

MillionBridges channels funds sent from Lebanese living abroad to their loved ones back home, in a safe, secure and regulated manner, using a system that is instantaneous, convenient and value preserving. Funds are kept in Euros, and are used to purchase foodstuffs and groceries from a set of partner-supermarkets by using only a mobile phone and ID card.

2. How can I send funds to my family in Lebanon?

You open an account through the MillionBridges website and set the beneficiary in Lebanon. Using your credit card, you fill your wallet, and send the funds.

3.What will it cost me?

You will be charged a processing fee of 2%. Recipients are not charged any commission; they receive the full amount sent. MillionBridges operates only in Euros. If your credit card is non-Euro based, your bank might charge you a conversion fee from your currency to Euros.

4. How fast is the process?

It is instantaneous. Once your wallet is filled, and the funds sent, the beneficiary can immediately use them to purchase groceries at partner supermarkets.

5. Can I return the funds that are in the wallet if I decide to?

If for any reason you decide to retrieve the available funds from your wallet, you can do so. You will be charged a return processing fee of 2%.

6.How is the Euro / LBP rate decided?

It is set by partner supermarkets, and supervised by MillionBridges to ensure that recipients receive a fair and advantageous exchange rate.

7.How can I track the available funds and the transactions that were made from my account?

You can log in at any time to your user account at MillionBridges, and check all the details and transactions that have taken place.

8.Who can use MillionBridges?

Any person wishing to send funds to family or friends in Lebanon. You will need a valid ID and credit cards, and you will need to pass our security and Anti Money Laundering filters. Recipients in Lebanon must be Lebanese citizens, with valid ID cards.

9.Can I send any amount through MillionBridges, and to anyone?

Due to financial regulations, a maximum amount per transaction is established. There is also a maximum number of recipients you can send funds to. Recipients have a maximum number of senders they can receive money from.

The company is managed by experts in the financial, legal, software development and consumer marketing fields. MillionBridges is apolitical and non-denominational.
